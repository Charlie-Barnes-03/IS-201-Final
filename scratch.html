Inside a Runner's Mind

My Running Journey

Growing up, I played a bunch of sports. Soccer was my favorite, and I played it the most, so I ran a lot for that. I always told the people around me that I didn't like running just to run. I even tried doing track in 9th grade, but I just didn't enjoy it. Once I got to college I started dating a girl who liked to run, so I picked it up slowly. We decided to run a marathon at the end of the summer, so I started running more. I found it to be a good way to take care of my body and relieve the stress of school.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  1. Running is exercise, and exercise is healthy
  2. See beautiful places at beautiful times of year
  3. Meet new people and strengthen existing friendships
  4. Feel accomplished at any level

Cons

  1. Doesn't have to be, but can get expensive:
    • Race fees: Any kind of organized race will most likely have an entry fee
    • Shoes: You might think you only need one pair of running shoes. Think again! If you want to run multiple times a week it's advised that you get at least 2 pairs
    • Clothes and gear: The right clothes and gear make running more comfortable and convenient
    • Feul: If you choose to run big race, you'll have to start feuling your body while training
  2. Can be hard on your body
  3. Can be time consuming and weather dependent
